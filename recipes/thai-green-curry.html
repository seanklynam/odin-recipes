Thai green curry

Straightforward recipe for a classic Thai green curry

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp Thai green curry paste
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1–2 thick stalks lemongrass,
  • 750g skinless, boneless chicken chicken breast,
  • 6–8 kaffir lime leaves
  • 400ml tin coconut milk
  • Thai fish sauce
  • handful of coriander
  • ½–1 lime, juice only
  1. Heat the oil in a wok or large frying pan. Add the green curry paste and sugar and cook over a fairly high heat for about a minute, stirring with the lemongrass, if using.
  2. Reduce the heat slightly and stir in the chicken pieces and lime leaves or zest until coated in the paste.
  3. Add the coconut milk, fish sauce or soy sauce and bring to a simmer, cooking for 25–30 minutes until thickened slightly.
  4. Stir in the coriander and lime juice. Check for seasoning, adding more fish sauce or soy sauce if needed.
  5. The curry is now best left to sit for a few minutes so the sauce becomes creamier.
  6. Serve with lots of fragrant Thai jasmine rice.
